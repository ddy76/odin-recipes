Description:

This vegetarian chili is a truly comforting and satisfying meal that even devout meat-eaters would appreciate. Its flavor is a masterclass in slow-simmered complexity, with a smoky, savory depth from the chili powder and cumin, balanced by the bright tang of fire-roasted tomatoes. The texture is where it really shines, proving that chili can be hearty without meat. The combination of kidney and black beans, along with finely chopped vegetables like peppers and onions, creates a thick, rustic, and fulfilling stew that holds its own when topped with cheese or sour cream.

The recipe is remarkably easy to follow, making it a perfect weeknight dinner despite its rich flavor. It is a testament to how pantry staples can be elevated into something special and nourishing. For those worried about a lack of heft, the generous portion of beans and vegetables leaves no doubt that this is a complete and filling meal. This chili is not only delicious but also a great, budget-friendly option for anyone craving flavorful comfort food.

Prep Time: 20 Minutes, Cook Time: 1 hr, Total Time: 1hr 20mn, Servings: 8-10

Ingredients

  • 1/2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 white or yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 1 poblano pepper, chopped
  • 2 jalapeno peppers, seeded and chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded and chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 stalks celery, finely chopped
  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, finely chopped, or more to taste
  • 2 (14.5 ounce) cans fire roasted diced tomatoes
  • 1 quart vegetable broth
  • 2 (15 ounce) cans black beans
  • 1 (15 ounce) can chili beans
  • 1 (15 ounce) can refried beans

Directions

    Step 1
    Heat oil in a large Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook 2 minutes, stirring often until it begins to soften. Add poblano pepper, jalapeno pepper, bell pepper, carrots, celery, and sweet potatoes and stir to combine. This is a comit to change as a test.
    Step 2
    Add chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, salt, pepper, and chipotle in adobo and cook, stirring occasionally, 3 minutes.
    Step 3
    Add tomatoes and stock and bring to a boil. Add black beans and chili beans, reduce heat, cover, and simmer until veggies are tender, about 25 minutes. Stir in refried beans and return to a simmer. Cover and cook 15 more minutes, stirring occasionally.
